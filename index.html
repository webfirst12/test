Tairel

Cedrela toona Roxb.

Cedrela toona Roxb., known locally as Tairel, is a versatile and important tree in Manipur. It grows to a medium or large size and has distinctive brown or grey scaly bark. Its leaves are long and often asymmetrical, and its seeds have wings on the ends.

Khujang

Impatiens balsamina L.

Impatiens balsamina L., known as Gul Mehendi in Hindi and Khujang in Manipuri, is an annual herb commonly called garden balsam or rose balsam. Native to southern Asia, it typically grows to 1-2.5 feet tall. The plant is named for its "impatience" in releasing seeds from its ripe pods. The leaves are spirally arranged, with deeply toothed edges, and the flowers come in various colors, including pink, red, and white. Pollination is mainly done by bees, insects, and birds.

Heibi

Meyna spinosa Roxb.

Meyna spinosa Roxb. (commonly known as Heibi in Manipuri) is a spiny, typically small tree or large shrub, reaching a height of 5-8 meters. The plant is characterized by its busy branches and dark-colored, nearly smooth bark. Its spines are axillary or supra-axillary, straight, sharp, and range from 5 to 40 mm in length. The leaves are membranous, ovate or elliptic-oblong, slightly pubescent above, densely pubescent beneath, with bluntly or obtusely acuminate apex, entire margin, and rounded or acute base. The flowers, which are pale green or greenish white with a faint fragrance, are crowded into fascicles and have short peduncles, pedicels, and petioles. The plant typically flowers from April to May, and its yellowish, subglobose drupe fruits appear from August to December .
