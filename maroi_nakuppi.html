Maroi Nakuppi

Allium tuberosum Rottler ex Spreng.

Culinary Use:

Maroi Nakuppi is utilized as a replacement for onion, garlic, and ginger in Manipuri cuisine. Its tender leaves are consumed raw, cooked, or boiled and are used in various local dishes, salads, and soups. It adds a unique flavor resembling garlic.

Medicinal Properties:

The herb is believed to have medicinal benefits such as improving digestion, stimulating appetite, reducing body weight, fatigue, high blood pressure, and treating kidney and liver problems. Externally, it is applied to treat insect bites, cuts, and wounds.

Nutritional Value:

Maroi Nakuppi is rich in vitamins A, C, and K, as well as carbohydrates, proteins, minerals like calcium, potassium, and zinc. Its organic sulfur compounds provide characteristic flavors and contribute to human health benefits.

Phytopharmacology:

Research indicates various pharmacological activities of Allium tuberosum, including antioxidant, antimicrobial, hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, and anti-diabetic properties. It contains bioactive compounds like flavonoids and polysaccharides that contribute to these activities.

Scope for Innovation:

There is potential for biotechnological interventions to enhance the cultivation, nutritional content, and medicinal properties of Allium tuberosum. Further research and commercial cultivation could lead to the development of value-added products and nutraceutical foods.
