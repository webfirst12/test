Tairel

Cedrela toona Roxb.

Cedrela toona Roxb., known locally as Tairel, is a versatile and important tree in Manipur. It grows to a medium or large size and has distinctive brown or grey scaly bark. Its leaves are long and often asymmetrical, and its seeds have wings on the ends.

Traditional Uses

Pest Control: The leaves of Tairel, when fermented with cow urine, create a natural spray used by farmers to protect rice crops from pests and diseases.

Medicinal Uses

The Chakma community uses a boiled leaf mixture to treat stomach issues.

Local folklore prescribes Tairel leaves for treating vomiting, skin diseases, chicken pox, and diarrhea

In Pakistan, the dried leaves are used to manage diabetes and skin problems.

In Andhra Pradesh, the bark is used to care for skin and treat skin conditions.

Cultural and Practical Uses: The wood from Tairel is used to make durable rosaries, commonly sold in Manipur markets. The leaves are also used in local festivals and religious practices.

Research and Benefits

Studies have shown that extracts from Tairel leaves can help lower blood sugar levels in diabetic rats, suggesting potential benefits for managing diabetes.

Community and Cultural Importance

The Chakpa community, one of the oldest in Manipur, incorporates Tairel into their daily lives and traditions. The tree is an essential part of their homes and their traditional medicine.

Potential for Sustainable Development

Tairel offers opportunities for creating low-cost, natural products for pest control and food preservation, which can support local economies and promote sustainable practices in rural areas.
